Singapore University of Technology and Design

Since May 2013, I have been an undegraduate at the Singapore University of Technology and Design. The first year was spent studying a variety of general modules, which every student goes through. By the end of the first academic year, I began developing an interest in Systems Optimisation. This coupled with my pre-existing interest in Economics, motivated my decision to join the Engineering Systems Design Pillar.

Engineering Systems Design entails the analysis, design, modelling and optimisation of macro systems, whether they are large scale transport systems, company supply chains, or financial markets. The core learning outcomes provide a very broadly applicable set of technical and management skills. The main Modules studied for my Sophomore and Junior year at SUTD were, Optimisation, Probabilistic Simulation, Operations Management, Finance Theory, Decision Analysis, Quality and Reliability, Project Management, Network Theory and Stochastic Modelling along with a large number of Projects.

In my final year at SUTD, I chose the Economics and Operations Research focus track. Modules were tailor made for this discipline and some were even post graduate and PhD level subjects. The core modules were, Advanced Optimisation, Advanced Stochastic Processes, Game Theory and The Analytics Edge. I also had the Final Year Project, or Capstone Project to tackle in the Final Year. The project undertaken was the building and deployment of a Online, Automated Financial Service called HUAT.
